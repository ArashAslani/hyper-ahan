# Content Strategy

**Project:** HyperAhan  
**Phase:** 2 — Business & UX Blueprint  
**Purpose:** Define how content supports SEO, trust, and sales.

Content exists to attract qualified traffic and convert readers into buyers.

---

## Content Principles

- Content must answer real buyer questions.
- Every content page must connect to a commercial path.
- Thin placeholder content must not be indexed.
- Blog is a business tool, not a publishing habit.
- Category and product pages are also content assets.
- SEO traffic is valuable only when it can convert.

---

## Role of Blog

Blog should attract users who are not yet ready to choose a product.

Primary blog roles:

- educate non-technical buyers
- explain technical product differences
- support calculator queries
- explain price volatility
- answer purchase process questions
- route readers to products/categories/contact

Blog must not become generic steel news unless the article has a clear buyer/conversion role.

---

## Role of Categories

Category pages are the main commercial SEO pages.

Each category should:

- explain the product family
- show current products/prices
- include filterable specs
- link to related articles
- link to relevant calculators
- include FAQ when useful
- provide expert CTA

Category content should help buyers compare and act, not only rank.

---

## Role of Product Pages

Product pages are high-intent conversion pages.

Each product page should:

- show product name/spec clearly
- show price/unit
- explain factory/brand/loading place
- show availability signal when reliable
- include add-to-cart and expert CTA
- link to related products
- link to relevant buying guide/calculator where helpful

Product SEO must not sacrifice conversion clarity.

---

## Internal Linking

Internal links should reflect buyer journeys.

Required link patterns:

- homepage -> categories
- homepage -> price/product pages
- category -> products
- category -> related articles
- category -> calculator
- product -> category
- product -> related products
- article -> category/product
- article -> calculator/contact
- calculator -> category/product/contact

Avoid orphan content.

---

Core clusters:

## Buying Guides

Buying guides should be practical.

Recommended structure:

1. Who this guide is for
2. What product solves which problem
3. Key specs to compare
4. Common mistakes
5. How price is calculated
6. Related products/categories
7. Expert CTA

Avoid long generic introductions.

---

## Educational Articles

Educational articles should serve discovery.

Good article types:

- “What is ...?”
- “How to choose ...”
- “How to calculate weight ...”
- “What affects price ...”
- “Common buying mistakes ...”

Each article needs:

- clear answer
- examples
- related commercial links
- CTA
- update policy when price-sensitive

---

## Article-to-Buyer Conversion Pattern

Every article should include:

- inline links to relevant categories/products
- a mid-article expert CTA for uncertain users
- related product/category block near the end
- calculator link if relevant
- final CTA that matches user intent

Example final CTA:

> Need help choosing the right rebar size? View today’s rebar prices or call an expert before submitting your request.

---

## Content Quality Rules

Content must be:

- original
- accurate
- useful
- specific to steel/construction buyers
- internally linked
- updated when market facts change
- free of unsupported claims

Do not publish generic AI filler.

---

## Content Governance

Before publishing future content:

- define target keyword/intent
- define related product/category
- define CTA
- define structured data need
- decide index/noindex
- review for business accuracy

---

## Content Priority

Priority order:

1. Category content for major product families
2. Product page specification clarity
3. Buying guides for rebar/beam/sheet/profile
4. Calculator support content
5. Purchase process/support content
6. Market analysis only when there is editorial capacity

Blog should not be implemented before the roadmap content phase, but its business role is now defined.
