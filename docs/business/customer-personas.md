# Customer Personas

**Project:** HyperAhan  
**Phase:** 2 — Business & UX Blueprint  
**Business objective:** Increase conversion from visitor to customer.

These personas define who the platform serves and how future UX, SEO, and frontend decisions should support them.

---

## Persona 1 — Small Contractor

**Profile:** Runs small residential/commercial jobs and buys steel repeatedly but not always in very large volume.

**Goals**

- Find today’s price quickly.
- Compare factories/sizes.
- Submit a request before prices change.
- Talk to a reliable expert.
- Track order status after confirmation.

**Pain points**

- Price volatility.
- Unclear final cost after loading/delivery.
- Too many technical tables on mobile.
- Worry about wrong product selection.
- Needs fast response during working hours.

**Buying behavior**

- Starts from Google or direct brand memory.
- Compares multiple suppliers.
- Calls when purchase value is meaningful.
- May add products to cart but expects expert confirmation.

**Device usage**

- Primarily mobile.
- May use desktop at office for larger comparisons.

**Search behavior**

- “قیمت میلگرد امروز”
- “قیمت تیرآهن ۱۴”
- “خرید میلگرد اصفهان”
- “قیمت ورق سیاه فولاد مبارکه”

**SEO intent**

- High commercial intent.
- Wants current price and supplier confidence.

**Decision factors**

- price freshness
- factory/brand
- loading place
- response time
- delivery confidence
- clear unit/quantity

**Trust factors**

- visible phone number
- expert consultation
- transparent price-lock explanation
- order tracking
- known factories/brands
- clear business/contact information

## Persona 2 — Procurement Manager

**Profile:** Buys for a workshop, company, or construction business and needs reliability more than visual simplicity.

**Goals**

- Compare prices across categories/factories.
- Get a pro-forma invoice.
- Confirm supply capability.
- Keep purchase records.
- Reduce risk of wrong specs.

**Pain points**

- Unclear stock/availability.
- Need for official documentation.
- Price changes between inquiry and confirmation.
- Hard-to-verify suppliers.
- Repeating the same details by phone.

**Buying behavior**

- Uses category tables and search.
- Reads specifications carefully.
- Calls after shortlisting.
- May return to same supplier if response and documentation are reliable.

**Device usage**

- Desktop during office hours.
- Mobile for follow-up and status checks.

**Search behavior**

- “قیمت پروفیل ۴۰ در ۴۰”
- “قیمت ورق ST37”
- “پیش فاکتور آهن آلات”
- factory/brand-specific searches

**SEO intent**

- Commercial and comparison intent.

**Decision factors**

- price accuracy
- invoice speed
- supply certainty
- support quality
- delivery timing
- company credibility

**Trust factors**

- business identity
- clear order process
- expert names/contact
- quality/control messaging
- support after submission
- consistent status updates

## Persona 3 — Non-Technical Individual Buyer

**Profile:** Individual renovating/building a small property; may not know exact steel terminology.

**Goals**

- Understand what product is needed.
- Avoid buying the wrong item.
- Ask an expert quickly.
- Submit a simple request without complex forms.

**Pain points**

- Steel product names and grades are confusing.
- Tables feel intimidating.
- Unsure how quantity/unit works.
- Fear of being overcharged.
- Does not know whether to call or checkout.

**Buying behavior**

- Starts with broad Google questions or articles.
- Needs educational content before product selection.
- Calls earlier than professional buyers.
- May abandon if too many fields appear.

**Device usage**

- Almost entirely mobile.

**Search behavior**

- “برای ساختمان چه میلگردی بخریم”
- “فرق تیرآهن ۱۴ و ۱۶”
- “محاسبه وزن میلگرد”
- “قیمت آهن کیلویی”

**SEO intent**

- Informational moving toward commercial.

**Decision factors**

- simple explanations
- calculator help
- expert CTA
- clear next step
- trust and reassurance

**Trust factors**

- plain-language guidance
- phone/WhatsApp availability
- clear process after request
- visible support
- no hidden commitment before expert confirmation

## Persona 4 — Engineer / Technical Advisor

**Profile:** Helps select specifications for a project and may influence purchase but not always complete checkout.

**Goals**

- Verify specs, weights, standards, and factories.
- Compare alternatives.
- Share products/articles with buyer.
- Use calculators and reference content.

**Pain points**

- Poor technical data.
- Missing weight/standard details.
- Inconsistent product naming.
- No direct links to related specs/articles.

**Buying behavior**

- Uses search and category filters.
- Reads technical article content.
- May send product links to contractor/procurement.
- Values structured specs more than marketing.

**Device usage**

- Desktop and mobile.

**Search behavior**

- “جدول وزن میلگرد”
- “مشخصات ورق ST37”
- “وزن تیرآهن ۱۴”
- product standard queries

**SEO intent**

- Informational and technical-commercial.

**Decision factors**

- data accuracy
- technical completeness
- product comparison
- downloadable/shareable info in future

**Trust factors**

- consistent specifications
- transparent sources
- expert support
- related technical articles

## Persona 5 — Workshop / Small Business Owner

**Profile:** Buys profile, pipe, sheet, wire rod, or accessories for fabrication or small manufacturing.

**Goals**

- Find repeat purchase items quickly.
- Compare current price.
- Reorder or ask for quote fast.
- Track order and delivery.

**Pain points**

- Repeated searching for the same items.
- Need to compare units and thickness/size.
- May buy outside standard building categories.
- Wants fast phone confirmation.

**Buying behavior**

- Searches by dimensions and product type.
- Uses recently viewed/popular products.
- Calls after shortlisting.
- May become repeat customer.

**Device usage**

- Mobile in workshop.
- Desktop for larger orders.

**Search behavior**

- “قیمت قوطی ۴۰*۴۰”
- “قیمت لوله داربست”
- “قیمت ورق روغنی”
- “پروفیل صنعتی قیمت”

**SEO intent**

- High commercial intent with technical filters.

**Decision factors**

- dimensions
- availability
- price/unit clarity
- loading/delivery practicality
- repeat speed

**Trust factors**

- recently viewed/reorder paths
- consistent product naming
- expert phone support
- clear delivery status

## Persona 6 — Returning Customer

**Profile:** Has already submitted orders and returns to check status or buy again.

**Goals**

- Track orders.
- Repeat previous product searches.
- Access profile/address.
- Contact expert quickly.

**Pain points**

- Hard to find previous order.
- No status clarity.
- Re-entering details repeatedly.
- Not knowing whether an order is confirmed, cancelled, or completed.

**Buying behavior**

- Uses direct navigation.
- May start from profile/orders instead of homepage.
- Values continuity and trust.

**Device usage**

- Mobile-first.

**Search behavior**

- branded/direct searches
- saved links
- direct app/browser bookmarks

**SEO intent**

- Low SEO acquisition; retention/conversion intent.

**Decision factors**

- order visibility
- reduced data entry
- fast support
- trust from previous delivery

**Trust factors**

- reliable status timeline
- clear support path
- preserved address/order context
- transparent cancellation/confirmation states

## Persona Prioritization

Primary for MVP conversion:

1. Small Contractor
2. Procurement Manager
3. Non-Technical Individual Buyer

Secondary but important:

4. Workshop / Small Business Owner
5. Engineer / Technical Advisor
6. Returning Customer

The platform must be simple enough for Persona 3 and credible enough for Personas 1 and 2.
